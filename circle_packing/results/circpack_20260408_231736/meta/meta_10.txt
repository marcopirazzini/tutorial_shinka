# GLOBAL INSIGHTS SCRATCHPAD

The following are global insights about optimization approaches and their effectiveness:

## Successful Algorithmic Patterns

- **Multi-Start Initialization + Joint Optimization**  
  Programs Generation 6 (“Multi-start adaptive circle packing”, sum = 2.58) and Generation 7 (same with lightweight SA refinement, sum = 2.58) combine three distinct seeds—concentric rings, hexagonal grid, and greedy max-space—with a joint SLSQP optimize step. This multi-start + global solver pipeline outperforms any single-seed approach (e.g. Gen 1 hex-grid + SLSQP at 1.94).

- **Aggressive Adaptive Radii Sweeps**  
  The `compute_adaptive_radii` routine in Gen 6/7 performs 15 forward–backward passes of proportional shrinking, driving tight non-overlap fits and enabling the jump from ~1.96 (Gen 5) to 2.58. Earlier shrink-only methods (Gen 0, Gen 2, Gen 3) plateaued below 1.85.

- **Radii-Boost Post-Processing**  
  After SLSQP, both Gen 6 and Gen 7 invoke a local “boost_radii” pass that grows each circle up to the next collision margin. This step yields incremental gains on top of the optimized solution, helping reach the record 2.58.

- **Margin-Slack Constraints in SLSQP**  
  The current best code adds a small wall/overlap margin (0.0005) in its constraint definitions, improving numerical robustness. Even Gen 1’s SLSQP scheme benefited from constraint slack (sum = 1.94) compared to zero-slack greedy methods.

## Ineffective Approaches

- **Pure Shrink-Only Heuristics**  
  Ring-based (Gen 0, sum = 1.82) and greedy anchored methods (Gen 2, sum = 1.78) rely solely on initial structure + proportional shrinking, leading to excessive radius reductions and sums well below 2.0.

- **Unconstrained Repulsive Objectives**  
  Both Gen 4 (“Hex-Jittered Grid with Repulsive Optimization”) and Gen 8 (“Hex-Init Force-Relax Packing”) scored 0.0 and failed validation. Their repulsive-potential minimization drifted circles into overlapping/infeasible configurations, indicating poor alignment between repulsion cost and maximal‐radius objectives.

- **Simple Local Search Without Global Refinement**  
  Ring-with-annealing (Gen 3, sum = 1.85) and structured relaxation (Gen 5, sum = 1.96) used center perturbations plus shrink-to-fit, but lacked a global optimizer. They improved over naive heuristics yet remained ~0.6 below the multi-start best.

- **Over-Rigorous Annealing**  
  Although Generation 7 added a 260-step SA refinement, it did not surpass Generation 6’s 2.58, suggesting diminishing returns and extra cost from SA when the multi‐start + SLSQP + boost pipeline is already saturated.

## Implementation Insights

- **Pipeline Modularity Accelerates Tuning**  
  Splitting the algorithm into clear stages—initialization, adaptive radii sweeps, SLSQP, radii boosting, optional SA—allowed incremental improvements at each stage. The best program’s code structure exemplifies this composability.

- **Adaptive Radii Ordering Matters**  
  The use of both forward and backward passes over circle indices in `compute_adaptive_radii` avoids biasing large vs. small indices, achieving more uniform shrinkage and higher total radii than single-pass shrinking.

- **Local Radii Growth vs. Global Solve**  
  Post-SLSQP boosting trades off a small overlap margin for extra radius without re-running expensive optimizers, squeezing out final improvements that simple shrink routines cannot.

- **Constraint Margins Enhance Feasibility**  
  Introducing a fixed margin (0.0005) in both wall and overlap constraints prevents numerical violations during SLSQP. This technique consistently yields valid packings at high density.

## Performance Analysis

- **Score Progression**  
  – Gen 0 (ring only): 1.82  
  – Gen 1 (hex-grid + SLSQP): 1.94  
  – Gen 3 (ring + annealing): 1.85  
  – Gen 5 (structured relaxation + hill-climb): 1.96  
  – Gen 6/7 (multi-start + adaptive radii + SLSQP + boost): 2.58  

- **Multi-Start Leap**  
  Transitioning from single-seed pipelines (best previous 1.96 in Gen 5) to a three-seed multi-start approach delivered a ~0.62 increase in sum of radii, demonstrating the high value of diversified initial layouts.

- **Diminishing Returns from SA**  
  Although SA can escape local optima, generation 7’s addition of 260 SA steps on top of Gen 6 did not raise the score beyond 2.58, implying that once adaptive-shrink + SLSQP has been applied across multiple starts, further random perturbations yield minimal benefit.

- **Failure Modes Show Algorithmic Misalignment**  
  Zero-score designs (Gen 4, Gen 8) confirm that objectives based purely on repulsive potentials or force-directed pressures do not reliably map to maximal‐radius packings, underscoring the necessity of direct constraints or adaptive radii calculations.

# META RECOMMENDATIONS

The following are actionable recommendations for the next program generations:

1. Introduce a fourth, randomized “Latin-Hypercube” seed into the multi-start pool. Generate one additional initialization by sampling 26 centres via a Latin-hypercube in [0.01,0.99]^2, then run it through the same adaptive-radii→SLSQP→boost pipeline. This will diversify your starting layouts—leveraging the proven benefit of multi-start—without discarding the current three strong seeds.

2. Make the 15-pass `compute_adaptive_radii` loop self-tuning. After each forward/backward sweep, track the total radius sum increase; terminate early if the gain falls below a small ε (e.g. 1e-5) or add extra passes when improvement persists. This retains the aggressive fitting that vaulted you to 2.58 while avoiding wasted work on passes that no longer contribute.

3. Perform a two-stage SLSQP solve with shrinking slack. First run your current SLSQP with 0.0005 wall/overlap margin to find a robust basin; take the optimized centres, reduce the margin to 0.0001, and re-solve (holding centres bounds tight). Evidence shows smaller slack tightens the final packing without sacrificing feasibility once you have a near-optimal layout.

4. Turn `boost_radii` into an iterative loop until convergence. Instead of a single pass over all circles, repeatedly apply the local growth logic—alternating forward and backward index orderings—until the overall sum of radii increases by less than a threshold (e.g. 1e-6). This extends the proven post-SLSQP gains by squeezing out every last bit of slack, as seen in Gen 6’s booster step.

5. Replace the full 260-step SA with a focused hill-climb on the k smallest radii circles. Identify the m circles with lowest radii-to-neighbour-distance ratio, jitter only those centres (say m=4) by a small Gaussian step, recompute max-radii, and accept improvements greedily for 50–100 iterations. This targeted refinement preserves most of SA’s escape capability at far lower cost and aligns with evidence that global SA yielded diminishing returns.
